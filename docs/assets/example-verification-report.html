SMAD3

SMAD3 has moderate evidence as a therapeutic target for Hypertrophic Scarring (3 papers, 266 citations)

Prior Knowledge Framing:

  • Evidence maturity: L3 in vivo support
  • Framing summary: SMAD3 is well-validated in vitro and in a rabbit ear in vivo model as a central mediator of TGF-β1-driven fibroblast activation and collagen deposition in hypertrophic scarring, with multiple pharmacological inhibitors (curcumin, ivermectin) showing preclinical efficacy, but no human clinical trial evidence exists in these abstracts.
  • Known facts:
    • SMAD3 phosphorylation downstream of TGF-β1 drives fibroblast activation, α-SMA expression, and collagen deposition in hypertrophic scar
    • SMAD3 (not SMAD2) mediates mechanical compression-induced MMP9 upregulation in hypertrophic scar fibroblasts
    • SMAD3 regulates collagen I/III mRNA expression, while procollagen-I secretion is controlled via a parallel TAK1 pathway independent of SMAD3
    • TLP modulates the SMAD2/SMAD3 phosphorylation balance, with TLP overexpression in hypertrophic scar tissue positively correlating with collagen synthesis
  • Attempted approaches:
    • Curcumin: inhibits SMAD3 phosphorylation and nuclear translocation in TGF-β1-stimulated fibroblasts; validated in rabbit ear hypertrophic scar model in vivo
    • Ivermectin: suppresses SMAD3 phosphorylation and reduces collagen I, α-SMA, and CCN2 in hypertrophic scar fibroblasts in vitro
    • siRNA-mediated SMAD3 knockdown: used in vitro to dissect SMAD3-specific roles in MMP9 expression and collagen regulation under compressive stress
    • Lentiviral TLP overexpression: modulates SMAD3 phosphorylation balance in dermal fibroblasts to study collagen synthesis
  • Remaining gaps:
    • No clinical trials targeting SMAD3 directly in hypertrophic scarring are described in these abstracts
    • Ivermectin and curcumin data are preclinical only; no human-subject efficacy data for SMAD3 inhibition in hypertrophic scar is present
    • The role of SMAD3 in the secretory/trafficking pathway for procollagen-I appears independent of collagen transcription, suggesting SMAD3 inhibition alone may be insufficient to fully block fibrosis
    • Contradictory TLP data: TLP inhibits SMAD3 phosphorylation yet is overexpressed in hypertrophic scar and promotes collagen synthesis, suggesting SMAD2 may compensate

Key Papers:

  • PMID:37400988 (2024) — Curcumin alleviates hypertrophic scarring by inhibiting fibroblast activation and regulating tissue inflammation.
  • PMID:33955514 (2021) — Ivermectin inhibits cell proliferation and the expression levels of type I collagen, α‑SMA and CCN2 in hypertrophic scar fibroblasts.
  • PMID:25166894 (2014) — Mechanical compression upregulates MMP9 through SMAD3 but not SMAD2 modulation in hypertrophic scar fibroblasts.

Pathway-Level Trial Note:

  • Pathway: TGF-β/SMAD3
  • Summary: Direct SMAD3 clinical programs remain sparse, but upstream TGF-β pathway drugs show a mixed translational record that is relevant to scar/fibrosis strategy.
  • P144 peptide (TGF-β1 inhibitor): Phase 2 skin-fibrosis program completed without visible follow-up development.
  • Fresolimumab (anti-TGF-β mAb): early clinical testing reached Phase 1 but programs were discontinued.
  • Galunisertib (ALK5 / TGF-βRI inhibitor): advanced to Phase 2 in oncology, with hepatotoxicity concerns limiting enthusiasm.
  • Interpretation: The pathway appears clinically actionable, but systemic TGF-β blockade has shown tractability and safety limits; that supports exploring local or modality-constrained SMAD3 strategies rather than assuming broad pathway inhibition is sufficient.

NOTCH1

NOTCH1 has moderate evidence as a therapeutic target for Hypertrophic Scarring (1 papers, 280 citations)

Prior Knowledge Framing:

  • Evidence maturity: L3 in vivo support
  • Framing summary: NOTCH1 is identified as an upregulated hub gene in keloid keratinocytes via transcriptomic profiling, and ASC subpopulations targeting NOTCH1-associated pathways prevented hypertrophic scar formation in a porcine model, but direct NOTCH1 inhibition has not been tested and no human evidence exists.
  • Known facts:
    • NOTCH1 expression is upregulated in keloid keratinocytes (KK) and implicated in KK development
    • NOTCH1 is identified as a hub gene in keloid pathogenesis via protein-protein interaction mapping and gene expression profiling
  • Attempted approaches:
    • Adipose-derived mesenchymal stromal cell (ASC) subpopulations that molecularly interact with NOTCH1 were identified and tested
    • ASC subpopulations enriched for inhibitory activity against keloid keratinocytes were administered in a miniature pig hypertrophic scar model, with effective prevention of scar formation
  • Remaining gaps:
    • Direct functional validation of NOTCH1 knockdown or inhibition in keloid keratinocytes is not reported in this abstract
    • The specific molecular mechanism by which ASCs interact with NOTCH1 to suppress KK development is not detailed
    • No human clinical data on NOTCH1-targeting strategies for hypertrophic scarring or keloids exists in this abstract

Key Papers:

  • PMID:41511717 (2026) — Single-cell transcriptomic profiling identifies therapeutic subpopulations of adipose-derived mesenchymal stromal cells for human keloid management.
